# Tone and Brand Voice Guidelines

This document defines how we communicate with customers. Consistency in voice builds trust and reinforces our brand identity.

---

## Core Brand Attributes

**[COMPANY NAME] is:**

- **Professional yet approachable** - We're experts, but we're human
- **Clear and concise** - We respect your time
- **Empathetic** - We care about your success
- **Solution-focused** - We're here to help, not to blame
- **Trustworthy** - We're honest, even when it's hard

**[COMPANY NAME] is NOT:**
- Overly casual or slangy
- Robotic or corporate-speak
- Dismissive or condescending  
- Vague or evasive
- Overly technical without explanation

---

## Voice vs. Tone

**Voice** = Consistent personality (always the same)
**Tone** = Emotional inflection (adapts to situation)

Our **voice** stays consistent: professional, helpful, clear.
Our **tone** adapts: more empathetic for frustrated customers, more celebratory for successes.

## Tone for Different Situations

### Happy Customers

**Situation:** Customer is satisfied or thanking you

**Tone:** Warm, genuine, not over-the-top

‚úÖ **Good examples:**
- "I'm so glad I could help!"
- "That's great news! Thanks for letting me know."
- "You're very welcome. Don't hesitate to reach out if you need anything else!"

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- "I'm absolutely thrilled!" (too much)
- "No problem." (dismissive)
- "That's what we're here for!" (clich√©)

---

### Confused Customers

**Situation:** Customer doesn't understand something

**Tone:** Patient, clear, encouraging (never condescending)

‚úÖ **Good examples:**
- "Great question! Here's how it works..."
- "I can see how that would be confusing. Let me break it down..."
- "No worries at all! This trips people up sometimes. Here's the simple version..."

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- "It's actually quite simple..." (implies they should have known)
- "As I mentioned before..." (makes them feel bad)
- "Obviously..." or "Clearly..." (condescending)

---

### Frustrated Customers

**Situation:** Customer is upset, angry, or repeatedly having issues

**Tone:** Empathetic, action-oriented, validating

**The Formula:**
1. Acknowledge their frustration
2. Apologize (if appropriate)
3. Take ownership
4. Explain what you'll do
5. Do it quickly

‚úÖ **Good examples:**
- "I completely understand your frustration. This should have worked smoothly, and I'm sorry it didn't. Let me fix this for you right now."
- "I can see this has been ongoing, and that's not acceptable. I'm making this my priority to resolve today."
- "You're absolutely right to be frustrated. Let me make this right."

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- "I understand your frustration, BUT..." (negates the empathy)
- "Calm down..." (dismissive)
- "Unfortunately..." (sounds defeatist)
- "As per our policy..." (hiding behind rules)
- "That's not my department..." (passing the buck)

**Never blame the customer, even subtly:**
‚ùå "You should have..."
‚ùå "If only you had..."
‚ùå "Next time, make sure to..."

---

### Urgent Situations

**Situation:** Time-sensitive issue affecting business

**Tone:** Calm, efficient, action-oriented

‚úÖ **Good examples:**
- "I understand this is time-sensitive. I'm prioritizing this right now."
- "Let me get this resolved immediately. Give me 2 minutes to check on this."
- "I'm on it. Here's exactly what I'm doing: [specific steps]"

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- "Let me see what I can do..." (vague)
- "I'll try..." (lacks confidence)
- Long explanations (save for later, act now)

---

### Delivering Bad News

**Situation:** Can't give customer what they want

**Tone:** Honest, empathetic, solution-focused

**The Formula:**
1. Be direct but kind
2. Explain why (briefly)
3. Offer alternative if possible
4. Show you care about their situation

‚úÖ **Good examples:**
- "I wish I could say yes to this, but our policy doesn't allow exceptions because [brief reason]. What I can do instead is [alternative]."
- "I totally understand why you'd want this. Unfortunately, that feature isn't available yet. Here's a workaround that might help: [solution]"
- "I don't have great news here - [bad news]. I know that's not what you wanted to hear. Let me see what else we can do..."

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- "No." (too blunt)
- "That's impossible." (sounds dismissive)
- "You can't..." (sounds like you're denying them)
- Long policy explanations before the answer

## Starting Conversations

**First message in a conversation:**

‚úÖ **Good:**
- "Hi [Name]! I'm here to help. What can I do for you today?"
- "Hello! How can I help you?"
- "Hi there! I can help with that. Let me take a look..."

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- "Dear Customer," (too formal)
- "Hey!" (too casual)
- Jumping straight to solution without greeting

**Use their name when provided:**
- First mention: "Hi Sarah!"
- Throughout: "Sarah, here's what I found..."
- NOT every single message (feels robotic)

---

## Ending Conversations

**Always end with an offer to help further:**

‚úÖ **Good:**
- "Is there anything else I can help you with today?"
- "All set? Let me know if you need anything else!"
- "Happy to help! Feel free to reach out if anything else comes up."

‚ùå **Avoid:**
- "Goodbye." (abrupt)
- "Have a nice day." (generic)
- Nothing at all (incomplete)

**After resolving an issue:**
- "Glad I could help! Anything else you need?"
- "Problem solved! Is there anything else I can do for you?"

**If escalating:**
- "[Specialist] will reach out within [timeframe]. In the meantime, is there anything else I can help with?"

## Quick Reference Checklist

Before sending any message, ask yourself:

- [ ] Is this clear and easy to understand?
- [ ] Did I acknowledge their situation/emotion?
- [ ] Am I being helpful, not just informative?
- [ ] Did I avoid jargon (or explain it)?
- [ ] Is my tone appropriate for their situation?
- [ ] Did I offer next steps or ask if they need more help?
- [ ] Would I appreciate receiving this message if I were them?

---

## When in Doubt

**Remember our brand mantra:**
*"Professional expertise with a human touch"*

If you're unsure whether your tone is right, read it out loud. If you wouldn't say it that way to a friend who was having this problem, revise it.
