# Escalation Guidelines for Support Agents

**Purpose:** This document defines when and how to escalate customer support issues beyond first-tier resolution.

---

## Quick Reference: When to Escalate

### üî¥ IMMEDIATE ESCALATION (Red Flags)

**Escalate within 15 minutes to supervisor:**

1. **Legal Threats**
   - Customer mentions lawyers, lawsuits, legal action
   - References to consumer protection agencies
   - Demands for legal settlement

2. **Safety Concerns**
   - Product caused injury or harm
   - Safety hazard or defect reported
   - Allergic reactions to products
   - Child safety issues

3. **Media/PR Risk**
   - Customer mentions contacting media/press
   - Threatens social media campaign
   - Claims to be journalist/influencer
   - Mentions BBB complaint or public review campaign

4. **Extreme Hostility**
   - Threats of violence
   - Extreme profanity directed at staff
   - Harassment of team members
   - Discriminatory language

5. **Data Breach Concerns**
   - Customer claims account was hacked
   - Suspicious activity indicating fraud
   - Report of exposed customer data
   - Security vulnerability reported

**Escalation Path:** Manager on duty ‚Üí Director of Support (if unavailable)
**Response Time:** Manager must respond to customer within 2 hours

---

## üü° PRIORITY ESCALATION (Yellow Flags)

**Escalate within 2 hours to senior agent or supervisor:**

1. **Policy Exceptions Over Threshold**
   - Refund requests >$500
   - Returns beyond 30-day window with compelling reason
   - Waiver of multiple fees
   - Custom solutions outside standard policy

2. **Repeat Issues**
   - Customer's 3rd+ contact about same issue
   - Multiple defective products to same customer
   - Pattern of shipping failures to same address
   - Escalated complaint that wasn't resolved

3. **Account Issues**
   - Request to access deceased customer's account
   - Business account requiring custom terms
   - Wholesale or bulk order inquiries
   - Partnership or collaboration proposals

4. **Complex Technical Issues**
   - Problem not covered in knowledge base
   - Requires coordination with IT/engineering
   - Affects multiple customers (potential system issue)
   - Integration or API problems

5. **VIP Customers**
   - High lifetime value (>$5,000 total purchases)
   - Influencer or business partner
   - Gold tier loyalty member or above
   - Customer flagged as VIP in system

**Escalation Path:** Senior Support Agent ‚Üí Team Lead ‚Üí Manager
**Response Time:** Escalated team member responds within 4 hours

---

## üü¢ STANDARD ESCALATION (Green Flags)

**Escalate within 24 hours to appropriate department:**

1. **Department-Specific Issues**
   - **Billing/Finance:** Billing errors, invoice requests, tax questions
   - **Product Team:** Feature requests, detailed product questions
   - **Marketing:** Partnership inquiries, affiliate questions
   - **IT:** Website bugs, technical account issues
   - **Warehouse:** Shipping errors, inventory questions

2. **Outside Policy Scope**
   - Situation not covered in any policy document
   - Edge case requiring judgment call
   - Request for information we don't typically provide
   - International shipping special circumstances

3. **Complaint Escalation**
   - Customer explicitly requests to speak with manager
   - Dissatisfied with resolution offered
   - Complaint about support experience
   - Negative feedback about company/product

**Escalation Path:** Appropriate department via email/ticket assignment
**Response Time:** Department responds within 24-48 hours

## How to Escalate

### Step 1: Document Everything

Before escalating, ensure ticket includes:
- **Complete timeline** of customer interactions
- **Customer information:** Name, email, order numbers, account history
- **Issue summary:** Clear description of problem
- **Actions taken:** What you've already tried
- **Customer tone/urgency:** Emotional state, urgency level
- **Supporting evidence:** Screenshots, photos, order details
- **Recommendation:** What you think should be done (if any)

### Step 2: Notify the Customer

**For immediate escalations (Red flags):**
> "I understand this is [serious/urgent]. I'm escalating this to my supervisor right now who will be in touch within [timeframe]. They will have full context of our conversation."

**For priority escalations (Yellow flags):**
> "I want to make sure you get the best resolution. I'm escalating this to [role/team] who has the authority to [specific action]. They'll review your case and respond within [timeframe]."

**For standard escalations (Green flags):**
> "To properly address this, I'm forwarding your request to our [department] team who specializes in [area]. You'll hear back within [timeframe]."

### Step 3: Tag the Ticket

Use these tags in the support system:
- **Priority level:** `immediate`, `priority`, `standard`
- **Category:** `legal`, `safety`, `refund-exception`, `technical`, etc.
- **Escalation type:** `supervisor`, `department`, `senior-agent`
- **Status:** `escalated-pending-response`

### Step 4: Follow Up

- **Red flags:** Check within 30 minutes that supervisor has seen it
- **Yellow flags:** Check within 4 hours for status update
- **Green flags:** Check next business day if no response

## What NOT to Do

### ‚ùå Don't Escalate These:

1. **Standard returns within policy** - Process these yourself
2. **Common technical issues in knowledge base** - Troubleshoot first
3. **Questions you can answer with existing documentation**
4. **Customer is just venting but satisfied with resolution**

## Escalation Authority Levels

### Tier 1 Support Agent (You)
**Can approve without escalation:**
- Returns within 30-day window
- Refunds up to $100
- Standard shipping fee waivers
- Replacement for defective items <$200
- Store credit up to $150
- Restocking fee waivers

### Tier 2 Senior Agent
**Can approve without further escalation:**
- Returns 31-45 days
- Refunds up to $300
- Policy exceptions (minor)
- Complex technical troubleshooting
- VIP customer accommodations
- Multiple fee waivers

### Tier 3 Supervisor/Manager
**Can approve:**
- Returns beyond 45 days (case-by-case)
- Refunds up to $1,000
- Significant policy exceptions
- Customer loyalty gestures (credits, gifts)
- Legal/PR sensitive responses
- Account closures and data requests

### Tier 4 Director
**Required for:**
- Refunds over $1,000
- Precedent-setting policy changes
- Legal matter responses
- Media inquiries
- Major compensation packages

## Special Scenarios

### The "Karen" Situation (Unreasonable Demands)

**Signs:**
- Demands that far exceed the issue's impact
- Threatens escalation to corporate, BBB, media as first resort
- Claims to "know the CEO" or "have connections"
- Previous history of similar complaints

**How to handle:**
1. Stay professional and empathetic
2. Clearly explain what you CAN do
3. Don't make promises beyond policy
4. Escalate if they insist, but document unreasonable demands
5. Let supervisor determine if customer is attempting fraud/abuse

### The Legitimate but Angry Customer

**Signs:**
- Frustration is proportional to the problem
- Has tried to resolve through proper channels
- Valid complaint about service failure
- Reasonable desired outcome

**How to handle:**
1. Acknowledge their frustration specifically
2. Apologize sincerely for the failure
3. Offer immediate action steps
4. Escalate to get them extra compensation/accommodation
5. Follow up personally to ensure resolution

### The Serial Returner

**Signs:**
- Returns >40% of purchases
- Pattern of using items then returning
- High-value returns frequently
- Always at end of return window

**How to handle:**
1. Process returns according to policy
2. Do NOT accuse customer of fraud
3. Flag account for manager review with pattern documentation
4. Manager may implement account restrictions
5. Let manager have conversation if restrictions needed

### The Potential Lawsuit

**Signs:**
- Mentions injury, damages, financial loss
- Requests incident reports or documentation
- Asks for company insurance information
- Uses legal terminology

**How to handle:**
1. DO NOT admit fault or liability
2. Express concern: "I'm sorry this happened"
3. DO NOT speculate on outcomes
4. Immediately escalate to manager
5. Manager will loop in legal team
6. Follow instructions - these cases are sensitive

## After Escalation

### Your Responsibilities:

1. **Monitor the ticket** until resolution
2. **Learn from outcome** - if similar issue arises, you'll know how it was handled
3. **Update knowledge base** if this revealed a gap
4. **Follow up with customer** (if supervisor doesn't) to ensure satisfaction

### Questions to Ask Yourself:

- Could I have resolved this without escalating?
- Did I provide all necessary information for quick resolution?
- What would I do differently next time?
- Should this scenario be added to training/knowledge base?

---

## Monthly Escalation Review

Track your escalations:
- **Appropriate escalations:** Issues that truly required higher authority
- **Learning opportunities:** Cases where you could have resolved with more knowledge
- **Excellent catches:** Times you correctly identified serious issues early

**Goal:** Reduce unnecessary escalations while maintaining high catch rate for serious issues.
